Using the form

You can enter as many plant records as you need. To add another record click the 'Add a plant' button.

If you are currently online then the entries will be saved as you go, automatically. Otherwise the records will be remembered on your device, but you will need to click 'save all' (on the Your lists menu) when you have a network connection again.

To delete a plant record, find it in the list and click the red 'bin' icon.

Identifying your plants
Plant names

If possible, please enter the scientific or common name of the plant, but don't worry if you don't know the full details. The list of suggested names includes a very wide range of both native and horticultural plants, but if the name you need isn't on the list then you can still type it in.

Remember to record only plants that are in flower. Plants with only buds or seed heads don't count and nor do ferns!

Please record only flowers growing in wild places. You can include weeds in gardens, lawns or verges, but cultivated plants in or near gardens behave very differently so we'd like to keep them out of our results.

Photos

Photos of the plant will help us confirm your record. Please provide a picture showing the whole plant, but it will also help us if you can provide close-up views of the flowers and leaves.

Locating your finds

If you are using a mobile device in the field then each record should be assigned a GPS-based geo-reference automatically.

Alternatively you can type a grid-reference (Ordnance Survey or OSI grid-reference) in the grid-reference field or click on the map.

By default, records will be automatically assigned the location you gave for the survey, so it's not required to enter individual grid-references.
